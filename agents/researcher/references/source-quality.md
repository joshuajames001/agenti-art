# Source Quality Reference Guide

## Tier 1 — High Credibility (cite directly)
- Government and official institutional websites (.gov, .edu)
- Peer-reviewed journals and academic publications
- Official company documentation and press releases
- Established news organizations with editorial standards
- Primary data (surveys with methodology disclosed, official statistics)

## Tier 2 — Medium Credibility (cite with note)
- Industry publications and trade press
- Well-known technology blogs (TechCrunch, Wired, Ars Technica)
- Wikipedia — use as a starting point, verify claims elsewhere
- Company blogs — useful for product info, biased for comparisons
- LinkedIn articles from domain experts

## Tier 3 — Low Credibility (use as leads only, never cite as fact)
- Anonymous forum posts (Reddit, Hacker News)
- Undated articles
- Sites with obvious commercial bias without disclosure
- AI-generated content without primary source links
- Social media posts

## Red Flags
- No author listed
- No publication date
- No sources cited for specific claims
- Domain registered recently (check whois)
- Excessive ads or sponsored content mixed with editorial
